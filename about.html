About Me

I’m a first-generation Latina engineer who loves solving complex problems and building systems that bridge hardware and software. When I’m not working on projects, you can usually find me at the gym or gaming.

My path into engineering wasn’t traditional. Before pursuing my degree, I worked a variety of non-engineering jobs—from fast food and food prep to scanning tickets at a music venue and working as a teller at a currency exchange. Those experiences taught me adaptability, responsibility, and how to work with people from all backgrounds.

I began my academic journey at City Colleges of Chicago, starting at Malcolm X College where I initially explored art and general coursework (I also love to draw). After some time, I realized I wanted to pursue engineering, but I hadn’t been taking the right classes. I restarted my path at Wilbur Wright College, where I fully committed to engineering.

At Wright College, I met mentors who played a huge role in shaping my path, including Dr. Espiritu, Dr. T, and Bridget. With their guidance, I earned my Associate’s degree in Electrical Engineering. During that time, I discovered a growing interest in the software side of systems, which led me to pursue a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Engineering at Illinois Institute of Technology.

Balancing multiple jobs while taking a full course load made my journey challenging, but it also strengthened my time management, resilience, and determination. Today, I’m passionate about embedded systems, hardware-software integration, and building real-world solutions that make an impact.

Now that I’ve earned a degree I once thought was out of reach, I’m actively seeking opportunities where I can continue learning and grow into a successful engineer. I’m especially interested in systems-focused roles and enjoy working on projects that integrate both hardware and software. I’m also open to exploring other engineering roles where I can apply my skills and continue developing professionally. I’m looking for a position that will challenge me, allow me to keep building my skills, and give me the opportunity to contribute and grow.
